2. Inside the sector card div (`<div class="classified-border bg-bg-surface p-4">`), add a conditional img element BEFORE the existing flex container with the h3 and star rating:
```astro
{sector.coverPhoto && (
  <img
    src={`/images/cards/${sector.coverPhoto.replace(/\.(jpg|jpeg|png)$/i, '.webp')}`}
    alt={`${sector.name} sector terrain`}
    width="600"
    height="338"
    loading="lazy"
    decoding="async"
    class="w-full aspect-video object-cover mb-3 -mx-4 -mt-4 px-0"
  />
)}
```

IMPORTANT layout details:
- Use `width="600" height="338"` attributes for CLS prevention (browser reserves space before image loads)
- Use `loading="lazy"` and `decoding="async"` -- these cards are below the fold
- The negative margin classes `-mx-4 -mt-4` make the image bleed to the card edges (the card has `p-4`). Add `w-[calc(100%+2rem)]` to compensate for the negative margins, or simply use a wrapper approach. Test which looks better -- if negative margins are tricky with Tailwind v4, wrap the entire card content differently: put the img outside the padding div, or restructure the card to have `p-0` on the outer div and `p-4` on an inner content div below the image.
- Simpler approach if negative margins are complex: just keep the image inside the padding and let it have natural margins. The `w-full aspect-video object-cover mb-3` classes are sufficient for a clean look.
